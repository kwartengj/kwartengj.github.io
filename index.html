About Dr. Kwarteng and His Commitment to Ethical AI

I am a Lecturer in Artificial Intelligence at the School of Computing and Communications (C&C), part of The Open University. My research agenda is driven by a deep commitment to developing ethical and inclusive AI systems that actively address gendered and racial harms within digital spaces. I am dedicated to advancing equity, diversity, and trust in technology.

My PhD research focused on the computational redefinition of intersectional hate speech, with particular emphasis on Misogynoirâ€”the unique anti-Black misogyny experienced by Black women. This work directly informs my current initiatives on digital safety. Before my current role, I was a Research Associate on the SAGE-RAI project, where I implemented Generative AI educational experiments to enhance learning practices and outcomes, aligning with the principles of Industry 5.0.

My academic journey includes a Master of Science in Information Systems and Operations Management from the University of Nottingham. My career is built on developing societally beneficial systems and leveraging technology for the greater good, particularly in combating digital harms and promoting social justice globally.

Pillars of Research and Expertise

My research program employs techniques from Machine Learning, Data Mining, and Computational Social Science to investigate the socio-technical dimensions of AI bias and harm.

Responsible AI & Social Justice

Developing ethical frameworks and systems that ensure AI models are Fair, Accountable, and Transparent (FAT). This research addresses systemic bias and actively promotes social equity through technology design.

Intersectional Hate Speech: Misogynoir

Focused work on identifying and mitigating complex, compounding forms of online abuse. My expertise is in Misogynoir (anti-Black misogyny), requiring advanced understanding of race and gender intersectionality.

Generative AI in Higher Education (GenAIEd)

Research into the responsible adoption and best practices for integrating GenAI tools, like LLMs, into educational contexts to improve personalization and learning efficacy ethically.
